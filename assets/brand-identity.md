# OJ Brand Identity — Visual Language & Logo Concept

OJ’s brand identity is built around the idea of **freshness, clarity, and morning energy**.  
The visual system reinforces the metaphor of starting your day with a glass of orange juice — bright, energising, and simple.

This document outlines the core elements of the OJ brand identity.

---

# 🍊 1. Brand Essence

**OJ = Fresh Start Workforce**

The brand should evoke:
- freshness  
- optimism  
- clarity  
- energy  
- simplicity  
- human warmth  

OJ is not corporate or cold.  
It is friendly, modern, and mobile-first.

---

# 🍊 2. Logo Concept

The OJ logo is built around a **stylised orange slice** that also resembles a **sunrise** — symbolising the start of the day and the start of action.

### **Core elements:**
- A circular mark divided into **10 segments**  
- Segments represent the 10 WFM capabilities  
- Segments can appear full or empty depending on task completion  
- Soft, rounded geometry  
- Clean, modern typography for the “OJ” wordmark  

### **Symbolism:**
- **Orange slice** → freshness, energy  
- **Sunrise** → new day, new tasks  
- **Segments** → clarity and structure  
- **Completion mechanic** → “drink your juice”  

### **Logo Variations:**
1. **Primary Logo**  
   - Icon + “OJ” wordmark  
   - Tagline optional: *Fresh Start Workforce*

2. **Icon-Only**  
   - 10-segment orange slice  
   - Used for app icon, favicon, avatars

3. **Horizontal Lockup**  
   - Icon on the left, “OJ” on the right

4. **Monochrome Version**  
   - For printing, embossing, small-scale use

---

# 🍊 3. Colour Palette

OJ’s palette is inspired by citrus tones and morning light.  
It should feel bright, warm, and energising — but not childish.

### **Primary Colours**
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|--------|
| Sunrise Orange | `#FF8A00` | Primary brand colour, segments, highlights |
| Citrus Yellow | `#FFD36E` | Gradients, accents, highlights |
| Peach Glow | `#FFB88A` | Backgrounds, soft UI elements |
| Deep Tangerine | `#FF6B00` | Buttons, calls to action |

### **Secondary Colours**
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|--------|
| Fresh Mint | `#A8F0C6` | Success states, positive feedback |
| Sky Blue | `#A7D8F0` | Calm backgrounds, info states |
| Coral Alert | `#FF6F61` | Alerts, warnings |

### **Neutral Palette**
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|--------|
| Charcoal | `#2E2E2E` | Text, contrast |
| Slate | `#3A3A3A` | Secondary text |
| Soft Grey | `#EDEDED` | Dividers, cards |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Clean backgrounds |

### **Accessibility Notes**
- All primary colours meet contrast guidelines when paired with charcoal.  
- Alerts use coral for visibility without harshness.  
- Gradients should be subtle and warm.

---

# 🍊 4. Typography

OJ uses modern, friendly typefaces that feel clean and approachable.

### **Primary Typeface (Wordmark & Headings)**
**Poppins**  
- Geometric  
- Rounded  
- Modern  
- Friendly  

### **Secondary Typeface (UI & Body Text)**
**Inter**  
- Highly readable  
- Perfect for mobile  
- Neutral and flexible  

### **Accent Typeface (Marketing)**
**Circular Std** or **Avenir Next Rounded**  
- Soft, warm personality  
- Great for taglines and hero text  

---

# 🍊 5. Iconography

OJ’s icons should be:
- rounded  
- minimal  
- line-based  
- friendly  
- consistent with the citrus theme  

Examples:
- small sunbursts  
- droplets  
- rounded checkmarks  
- soft arrows  

Avoid sharp edges or overly corporate icon sets.

---

# 🍊 6. App Icon Specification

The OJ app icon should be:
- the 10-segment orange slice  
- with a subtle sunrise gradient  
- centred, bold, and clean  
- recognisable at 32×32px  

### **App Icon States**
- **Default:** full, vibrant orange  
- **Completed:** pale, empty segments  
- **Notification:** small dot or glow  

This reinforces the “drink your juice” mechanic.

---

# 🍊 7. Brand Usage Guidelines

### **Do:**
- Use bright citrus tones  
- Keep layouts clean and spacious  
- Use rounded shapes and friendly typography  
- Reinforce the “fresh start” metaphor  
- Keep the tone warm and human  

### **Don’t:**
- Use harsh reds or blues  
- Overcomplicate the wheel  
- Add unnecessary gradients  
- Use corporate or stiff typography  
- Break the 10-segment structure  

---

# 🍊 8. Tone of Voice

OJ’s tone is:
- friendly  
- supportive  
- clear  
- energising  
- human  

Examples:
- “Here’s what needs your attention today.”  
- “You’re nearly finished with your OJ.”  
- “Great job — that segment is clear.”  

Avoid:
- jargon  
- corporate speak  
- overly technical language  

---

# 🍊 9. Summary

OJ’s brand identity is built around:
- a strong metaphor  
- a unique visual anchor  
- a warm, energising palette  
- friendly typography  
- a mobile-first mindset  

This identity makes OJ memorable, approachable, and distinct in the Workforce Management space.
